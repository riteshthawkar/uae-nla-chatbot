Chapter two tackles the formation of the United Arab Emirates, the issuance of the Interim Constitution, the Constituent Committee for the Drafting of the Permanent Constitution and the constitutional amendments thereto. Chapter three covers the legislature in the Constitution. Chapter four looks into the functions of the Union and its legislative powers. Chapter five explains the distribution of federal powers according to the ministerial portfolios. Chapter six examines the transfer of local administrations to the federal authority. In this book, the author reviews the evolution of judiciary in the UAE from tribal customs to modern positive laws and regulations, the organization of the local and federal judicial departments, the organizational structure of the Ministry of Justice, the types of courts, the required qualifications of judges, and famous judges before and after the Union in the period (1946-2000). The author relies on historical sources, previous studies, and a myriad of documents regarding legal agreements and treaties between the UAE and Arab and foreign countries.
